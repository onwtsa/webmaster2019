For students of Olathe Northwest not involved in the Engineering Academy, there are still plenty of Technology courses to participate and at Olathe Northwest. Many of these are taught by Mr. Sappingfield. Additionally, there are several technology courses sponsored by the engineering Academy that are open for anyone to enroll and participate in.

Technology Courses

Architecture Design 1:
this course provides a study of the fundamental principles of architectural design common to buildings, Bridges, homes, and other structural framework. Students May Design Homes or commercial buildings, construct models, create construction and presentation drawings and develop presentations ruling boards, models and 3D computer-generated renderings and animations.

Architecture Design 2:
this Advanced architectural drawing course will focus on communication skills used by professionals and various fields of architecture and design. Projects are developed to simulate real-world design problems using 3D computer-generated modeling. This course is recommended for any student interested in one of the many related fields of architecture or CAD technology.

Engineering Drafting / CAD 1:
this technical level course will help students learn to utilize CAD software as a design tool for engineering, as well as fundamentals of manual orthographic drawings. Several forms of graphic and electronic communication are explored. Students are exposed to graphic design, problem solving, research and design, and model construction. This class introduces students to 3D computer-generated modeling and Design.

Engineering Drafting / CAD 2:
This technical level drawing course will focus on communication skills used by professionals and various fields of engineering. 3D computer-generated modeling and design is the primary technical skill applied in this class. Students are presented with problem-solving situations involving the various types of communication systems. This course is recommended for any student interested in a career and one of the many related fields of engineering are CAD technology.

Introduction to Technology Education:
This introductory level, technology-based class will introduce students to Engineering Design, Architectural design and Production / Manufacturing technology. Students will learn fundamentals of manufacturing and and construction technology, create computer-aided drawings, production of prototypes, as well as research historical and future technology issues.

Material Science and Engineering:
students in this course will integrate Concepts from chemistry, physics, and math to understand how the atomic structure of matter determines the physical characteristics that make materials useful to designers. The course will come by and Hands-On design projects while learning the science behind the essential properties of metals, polymers, Ceramics, and composite materials. Students will gain an understanding of the importance of material science topics and all fields of engineering while also enhancing their engineering design skills.
